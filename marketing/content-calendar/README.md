# Content Calendar

Turn your product goals and audience notes into a realistic weekly content plan — no marketing team required.

## What It Does

Takes your product/feature, target audience, and conversion goals, then produces a structured content calendar mapping each day to a channel, format, hook, and call-to-action.

It structures ideas across four content types:
- **Awareness** — what problem you solve
- **Education** — how to use it / why it works
- **Proof** — case studies, testimonials, benchmarks
- **Conversion** — trial signup, demo booking, launch announcement

## How to Use

Tell it what you're launching, who it's for, and what channels you have (Twitter, LinkedIn, newsletter, blog). It builds a one-week or two-week calendar optimized for a small team.

## Output Example

```
| Day   | Channel    | Format       | Hook                          | CTA                     |
|-------|-----------|-------------|-------------------------------|-------------------------|
| Mon   | Twitter   | Thread       | "We spent 3 months on X..."  | Waitlist signup         |
| Tue   | LinkedIn  | Carousel     | "How we reduced Y by 70%"    | Download case study     |
| Wed   | Blog      | Article      | "The missing guide to Z"     | Newsletter subscribe    |
| Thu   | Twitter   | Demo video   | "Watch X handle Y in 30s"    | Try free tier           |
| Fri   | LinkedIn  | Text post    | "What we learned shipping X" | Follow for updates      |
```

## Pro Tips

- Be specific about your audience — "React devs at 50-person startups" beats "developers"
- Mention constraints upfront: tone, language, cadence, regional focus
- If a channel feels forced, ask it to skip that day rather than forcing filler content
## Why Use This Skill

Most solo developers and small teams either skip content planning entirely or spend hours debating what to post. This skill turns product goals into a realistic weekly schedule in minutes — with hooks, formats, and CTAs tailored to your channels.
